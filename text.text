        <h1>----WELCOME TO THIS PAGE----</h1>
        <P>We are pleased to introduce a series of government schemes aimed at empowering individuals and fostering
            community development. These initiatives are designed to address key areas such as education, healthcare,
            employment, and social welfare, ensuring inclusive growth and prosperity for all.</P>

        <h2>The List Of Schemes Is As Follows:-</h2>
        <h3> 1:One Nation One Subscription</h3>
        <img src="onesub.png">
        <p>As of January 18, 2025, the Government of India has introduced the One Nation One Subscription scheme, a
            centrally sponsored initiative led by the Ministry of Education. Launched in 2025, this scheme aims to
            establish a comprehensive digital library providing institutional access to global research across various
            academic disciplines. The initiative seeks to democratize knowledge, ensuring that students, educators, and
            researchers throughout the country can access a vast array of scholarly resources, thereby fostering an
            environment conducive to academic excellence and innovation</p>

        <h3>2: Subhadra Yojana</h3>
        <img src="subhadra-yojana.avif">
        <p>Another recent government initiative is the Subhadra Yojana, launched by the Government of Odisha on
            September 17, 2024. This women-centric welfare scheme aims to empower women aged 21 to 60 years by providing
            direct financial assistance. Eligible beneficiaries receive ₹10,000 annually in two installments of ₹5,000,
            disbursed on Raksha Bandhan and International Women's Day, over a five-year period. The funds are
            transferred directly into Aadhaar-linked bank accounts, promoting economic independence among women from
            economically weaker sections.
        </p>

        <h3>3:Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana</h3>
        <img src="pmfby.jpg">
        <p>Another recent government initiative is the Extension of the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY),
            approved on January 1, 2025. The Union Cabinet sanctioned the continuation of this flagship crop insurance
            scheme until the fiscal year 2025-26, with a total outlay of ₹69,515.71 crore. This extension aims to
            provide risk coverage for crops against non-preventable natural calamities, ensuring financial stability for
            farmers across the country. Additionally, a new Fund for Innovation and Technology (FIAT) with a corpus of
            over ₹824 crore has been established to enhance the scheme's implementation through advanced technology,
            improving transparency and expediting claim settlements.
        </p>

        <h3>4:PM Schools for Rising India </h3>
        <img src="school for rising.png">
        <p>Another recent government initiative is the PM Schools for Rising India (PM SHRI) scheme, launched by the
            Department of School Education and Literacy. This scheme aims to develop and upgrade select existing schools
            across India to showcase all components of the National Education Policy 2020. The PM SHRI schools will
            provide high-quality education in an equitable and inclusive manner, serving as exemplar schools and
            offering mentorship to other schools in their vicinity.
        </p>

        <h3>5:Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)</h3>
        <img src="pmkisan.jpg">
        <p>Another recent government initiative is the Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) scheme, a central
            sector program by the Government of India. This scheme provides financial assistance to small and marginal
            farmers, aiming to support their financial needs for procuring various inputs to ensure proper crop health
            and yields. Under PM-KISAN, eligible farmers receive direct income support of ₹6,000 per year, disbursed in
            three equal installments of ₹2,000 each, directly into their bank accounts. This initiative seeks to empower
            farmers, ensuring timely access to financial resources for agricultural needs.
        </p>n
