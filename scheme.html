Empowering individuals through various welfare programs.

One Nation One Subscription

As of January 18, 2025, the Government of India has introduced the One Nation One Subscription scheme, a centrally sponsored initiative led by the Ministry of Education. Launched in 2025, this scheme aims to establish a comprehensive digital library providing institutional access to global research across various academic disciplines. The initiative seeks to democratize knowledge, ensuring that students, educators, and researchers throughout the country can access a vast array of scholarly resources, thereby fostering an environment conducive to academic excellence and innovation

Subhadra Yojana

Another recent government initiative is the Subhadra Yojana, launched by the Government of Odisha on September 17, 2024. This women-centric welfare scheme aims to empower women aged 21 to 60 years by providing direct financial assistance. Eligible beneficiaries receive ₹10,000 annually in two installments of ₹5,000, disbursed on Raksha Bandhan and International Women's Day, over a five-year period. The funds are transferred directly into Aadhaar-linked bank accounts, promoting economic independence among women from economically weaker sections.

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana

Another recent government initiative is the Extension of the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY), approved on January 1, 2025. The Union Cabinet sanctioned the continuation of this flagship crop insurance scheme until the fiscal year 2025-26, with a total outlay of ₹69,515.71 crore. This extension aims to provide risk coverage for crops against non-preventable natural calamities, ensuring financial stability for farmers across the country. Additionally, a new Fund for Innovation and Technology (FIAT) with a corpus of over ₹824 crore has been established to enhance the scheme's implementation through advanced technology, improving transparency and expediting claim settlements.

PM Schools for Rising India

Another recent government initiative is the PM Schools for Rising India (PM SHRI) scheme, launched by the Department of School Education and Literacy. This scheme aims to develop and upgrade select existing schools across India to showcase all components of the National Education Policy 2020. The PM SHRI schools will provide high-quality education in an equitable and inclusive manner, serving as exemplar schools and offering mentorship to other schools in their vicinity.

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi

Another recent government initiative is the Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) scheme, a central sector program by the Government of India. This scheme provides financial assistance to small and marginal farmers, aiming to support their financial needs for procuring various inputs to ensure proper crop health and yields. Under PM-KISAN, eligible farmers receive direct income support of ₹6,000 per year, disbursed in three equal installments of ₹2,000 each, directly into their bank accounts. This initiative seeks to empower farmers, ensuring timely access to financial resources for agricultural needs.
